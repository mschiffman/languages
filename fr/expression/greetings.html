Greetings & leave-taking — hello, how are you, and the many ways to say goodbye

Textbooks give you bonjour and au revoir and stop there. Real French has a hello and a goodbye for every register — and a whole set of phrases for announcing that you're off. Grouped by moment:

Bonjour / Bonsoir  (standard, all-purpose)

"Bonjour !"

“Hello / Good morning” — through the daytime.


"Bonsoir !"

“Good evening” — from late afternoon on.


💡 You greet each person with bonjour only once a day; run into them again and it's a joking rebonjour ! or just re !


Salut / Coucou  (casual, with people you know)

"Salut !" /saly/

“Hi!” — informal, and it doubles as “bye.”


"Coucou !"

“Hiya!” — affectionate, for friends and family.



Ça va ?  (the default check-in — question and answer)

"(Comment) ça va ?"

“How's it going?” — reply with the same words: Ça va, merci. Et toi ?


"Comment vas-tu ? / Comment allez-vous ?"

“How are you?” — fuller, more formal (tu / vous).


Ça roule ? / Quoi de neuf ?  (very casual)

"Ça roule ?"

“All good?”


"Quoi de neuf ?"

“What's new?”


Typical replies  (from great to so-so)

"Ça roule, et toi ?"

“All good, you?”


"Tranquille."

“Chilled / all quiet.”


"Bof, ça va."

“Meh, okay.”



Au revoir / Salut / Ciao  (the parting words)

"Au revoir !"

“Goodbye” — all-purpose and polite.


"Salut !"

“Bye!” — informal (yes, the same word as “hi”).


"Ciao ! / Tchao !"

“Bye!” — casual, borrowed.


À bientôt / À plus / À demain…  (“see you…”)

"À bientôt !"

“See you soon!”


"À plus !" /a plys/

“See you later!” — the final s is usually pronounced when it stands alone as a goodbye.


"À toute !"

“See you in a bit!” (short for à tout à l'heure).


"À demain ! / À lundi ! / À la prochaine !"

“See you tomorrow / Monday / next time!”



Je te laisse / Je file  (the wind-up before goodbye)

"Bon, je vais y aller."

“Right, I'm going to head off.”


"Je te laisse."

“I'll let you go / leave you to it.”


"Je file. / Je me sauve."

“I've got to dash / run.”


"Faut que j'y aille."

“I've got to go.”


On se capte / On s'appelle  (staying in touch)

"On se capte."

“We'll catch up / be in touch.” — very casual.


"On s'appelle."

“We'll call each other.”


"Tiens-moi au courant."

“Keep me posted.”



À retenir

Salut works for both hello and goodbye; bonjour is once per person per day (then re). Ça va ? is your all-purpose check-in and its own answer, and the goodbyes stack neatly by distance in time: à toute → à plus → à demain → à bientôt → à la prochaine.
