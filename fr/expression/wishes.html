Wishes & set formulas — « bon appétit », « bon courage », « à tes souhaits »…

French runs on ritual well-wishing — a fixed phrase for meals, departures, effort, sickness and celebrations. Most start with bon(ne), which agrees with the noun that follows. Grouped below by the moment you'd use them:

Bonne journée / soirée / nuit…  (the standard parting wish)

"Bonne journée !"

“Have a good day!”


"Bonne soirée !"

“Have a good evening!”


"Bonne nuit !"

“Good night!” — only when someone's off to bed.


"Bonne fin de journée !"

“Enjoy the rest of your day!”


Bon week-end / Bonne semaine  (the gender follows the noun)

"Bon week-end !"

“Have a good weekend!”


"Bonne semaine !"

“Have a good week!”


💡 bon week-end (masc.) but bonne journée (fem.) — the adjective agrees with the noun, not with you.



Bon appétit !  (said by anyone at the table before eating)

"Bon appétit !"

“Enjoy your meal!”


Régale-toi !  (warm, casual — among friends and family)

"Régale-toi !"

“Enjoy! / Treat yourself!” — softer and friendlier than bon appétit.


Santé ! / Tchin-tchin !  (a toast)

"Santé !"

“Cheers!” — neutral.


"À la tienne ! / À la vôtre !"

“Cheers!” — tu / vous.


"Tchin-tchin !"

“Cheers!” — very casual. Custom: meet people's eyes as you clink.



Bon courage !  (for something hard or tedious to get through)

"Encore trois heures de route ? Bon courage !"

“Three more hours of driving? Hang in there!”


Bonne chance !  (when the outcome depends on luck)

"Le tirage est demain — bonne chance !"

“The draw's tomorrow — good luck!”


⚠️ The pair learners confuse: bon courage = effort you control (a long day, a move, an exam you've prepped); bonne chance = luck you don't (a draw, an audition result).


Merde ! (to performers)  (the French “break a leg”)

"Merde !"

Said to actors and performers before they go on. Wishing them bonne chance is thought to bring bad luck — and you're not supposed to reply merci.



Bon voyage / Bonne route  (route specifically when they're driving)

"Bon voyage !"

“Have a good trip!”


"Bonne route !"

“Drive safe! / Safe journey!” — used when they take the wheel.


"Bon retour !"

“Safe trip back!”


Bonnes vacances !  (vacances is always plural)

"Bonnes vacances !"

“Enjoy your holiday!”


Bonne continuation !  (parting from someone moving on)

"Bonne continuation !"

“All the best going forward.” — said when someone leaves a job, finishes a course, or heads off on a new path.



À tes souhaits !  (after someone sneezes)

"À tes souhaits ! / À vos souhaits !" /a te swɛ/

“Bless you!”


"À tes amours !"

Playful reply after a second sneeze.


Bon rétablissement !  (to someone unwell)

"Bon rétablissement !"

“Get well soon!”


Prends soin de toi / Repose-toi bien  (warm, caring)

"Prends soin de toi."

“Take care of yourself.”


"Soigne-toi bien."

“Look after yourself.” — when they're ill.


"Repose-toi bien."

“Rest well.”



Joyeux anniversaire !  (birthday)

"Joyeux anniversaire !"

“Happy birthday!” — also heard casually as bon anniversaire.


Bonne année / Joyeuses fêtes  (end-of-year)

"Bonne année !"

“Happy New Year!”


"Joyeux Noël !"

“Merry Christmas!”


"Joyeuses fêtes !"

“Happy holidays!”


Meilleurs vœux !  (the New Year card formula)

"Meilleurs vœux !" /mɛjœʀ vø/

“Best wishes!”



À retenir

The daily essentials: bonne journée, bon appétit, bon courage, bonne chance, à tes souhaits and, for travellers, bonne route. Two rules to keep straight: bon(ne) agrees with the noun that follows (bon week-end, bonne soirée), and courage ≠ chance — effort you control vs. luck you don't.
