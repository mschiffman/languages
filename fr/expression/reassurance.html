Reassuring & encouraging — « t'inquiète », « courage », « tu vas y arriver »

Two related jobs: calming someone who's worried, and cheering on someone facing a challenge. These are the warm, everyday phrases that carry a friendship. Grouped by what the moment calls for:

T'inquiète / Ça va aller  (don't worry)

"T'inquiète (pas)."

“Don't worry.” — t'inquiète on its own is very casual.


"Ça va aller."

“It'll be okay.”


"Ne t'en fais pas."

“Don't worry about it.”


"C'est pas la fin du monde."

“It's not the end of the world.”


Pas de panique / Respire  (steadying them)

"Pas de panique !"

“Don't panic!”


"Respire."

“Breathe.”


"Tout va bien se passer."

“Everything'll go fine.”


⚠️ Calme-toi can sound dismissive when someone's upset — respire / du calme land softer.



Vas-y / Tu peux le faire  (go for it)

"Vas-y !"

“Go for it!”


"Tu peux le faire !"

“You can do it!”


"Tu vas y arriver."

“You'll get there.”


"Lâche rien !"

“Don't give up!”


Courage / Accroche-toi  (keep going through the hard part)

"Courage !"

“Stay strong! / Keep going!”


"Accroche-toi !"

“Hang in there!”


"Tiens bon."

“Hold on / hang on.”


"Encore un effort !"

“One more push!”



Je suis là / Compte sur moi  (support)

"Je suis là."

“I'm here (for you).”


"Je suis là pour toi."

“I'm here for you.”


"Compte sur moi."

“You can count on me.”


"N'hésite pas si tu as besoin."

“Don't hesitate if you need anything.”



Tu gères / Ça va le faire  (confidence in them)

"Tu gères !"

“You've got this! / You're handling it!” — casual.


"Tu t'en sors très bien."

“You're doing really well.”


"C'est déjà bien."

“That's already good.”


"Ça va le faire."

“It'll work out.”



À retenir

To calm: t'inquiète, ça va aller (and respire rather than a dismissive calme-toi). To push someone forward: vas-y, tu vas y arriver, lâche rien, courage. And the warmest of all — je suis là.
