Expressions of Tiredness, Needing a Rest & Being Fed Up in French

Click the buttons below to find the most common ways to express being tired, needing a rest, and being fed up in French:

"Je suis crevé(e)." /kʀəve/

"I'm exhausted / wiped out." (casual)

"Je suis épuisé(e)." /epɥize/

"I'm exhausted."

"Je suis à plat."

"I'm drained / running on empty."

"Je n'en peux plus."

"I can't take it anymore." (physically)

"Je suis mort(e) de fatigue."

"I'm dead tired."

"J'ai la tête qui tourne."

"My head is spinning." (dizzy from exhaustion)



"J'ai besoin d'une pause."

"I need a break."

"Faut que je fasse une pause."

"I need to take a break." (casual, short for "Il faut que je fasse une pause")

"J'ai besoin de souffler." /sufle/

"I need to catch my breath / take a breather."

"J'ai besoin de décompresser."

"I need to decompress / unwind."

"J'ai besoin de me reposer."

"I need to rest."

"Je vais faire une sieste."

"I'm going to take a nap."



"J'en ai marre."

"I'm fed up." (very common, casual)

"J'en ai ras-le-bol." /ʀal(ə)bɔl/

"I've had it up to here."

"Ça suffit !"

"That's enough!"

"Je craque."

"I'm cracking / losing it."

"J'en peux plus, j'abandonne."

"I can't take it anymore, I give up."

"C'est trop, là."

"This is too much, seriously."
