Yes, absolutely. They have overwhelmed the traditional systems — that is, foster families or children's homes — with a huge number of children and teenagers, who in fact build up different kinds of suffering, particularly from family breakdowns. And I think that all children in care would benefit from this kind of personalized placement, one that combines access to care, the ongoing bond with the foster parents who remain present in their lives, and the bond with the biological parents when it's necessary and not harmful. So it's a whole tailor-made framework that also reflects real momentum. Along with Ky Rios Palma, who directed "Itinéraire d'un enfant placé," an earlier film that followed one child's journey through foster care, we wanted to look at a collective journey instead, and see how these very different children managed to support and encourage one another. We filmed for almost an entire year, and I think you can really see the progress — especially the progress Kaï made. She was dealing with a serious issue, and by the end she shows immense maturity. She really proves it, and then she reaches out to the younger children, offering them all her support and encouragement. They manage this thanks to the social workers who love them and are there for them around the clock — a profession that's so undervalued, so poorly paid.
