French Grammar Guide

Understanding Y and En

Master these essential French pronouns that replace phrases with à and de to speak more naturally and fluently.

Why Learn Y and En?

In French, y and en are adverbial pronouns that help avoid repetition. Instead of repeating a noun or phrase, French speakers replace it with these small but powerful words. They're used constantly in everyday conversation!

Y replaces:

  • à + noun/thing
  • Places (there)
  • Prepositions of place + noun

En replaces:

  • de + noun
  • Quantities and numbers
  • Partitive articles (du, de la, des)
Y

The Pronoun Y

Y typically means "there" or replaces phrases beginning with à (to, at, in).

1 Y Replaces Places (= there)

Without Y

Tu vas à Paris ?

Are you going to Paris?

With Y

Oui, j'y vais.

Yes, I'm going there.

Elle habite en France → Elle y habite.

She lives in France → She lives there.

Nous allons au cinéma → Nous y allons.

We're going to the cinema → We're going there.

Les clés sont sur la table → Les clés y sont.

The keys are on the table → The keys are there.

2 Y Replaces à + Thing (not a person)

Many French verbs are followed by à. When the object is a thing (not a person), use y to replace it.

penser à (to think about)

Tu penses à ton examen ? → Tu y penses ?

Are you thinking about your exam? → Are you thinking about it?

répondre à (to answer)

J'ai répondu à la question. → J'y ai répondu.

I answered the question. → I answered it.

s'intéresser à (to be interested in)

Elle s'intéresse à la musique. → Elle s'y intéresse.

She's interested in music. → She's interested in it.

participer à (to participate in)

Nous participons au projet. → Nous y participons.

We're participating in the project. → We're participating in it.

Common Expressions with Y

J'y vais !

I'm going! / I'm off!

Allons-y !

Let's go!

Vas-y !

Go ahead! / Go for it!

J'y suis !

I've got it! / I understand!

Il y a...

There is... / There are...

Ça y est !

That's it! / Done!

En

The Pronoun En

En replaces phrases with de, quantities, and expressions with partitive articles.

1 En Replaces de + Noun

When a verb is followed by de + noun (thing), replace it with en.

avoir besoin de (to need)

J'ai besoin d'argent. → J'en ai besoin.

I need money. → I need some.

avoir envie de (to want/feel like)

Tu as envie de chocolat ? → Tu en as envie ?

Do you want chocolate? → Do you want some?

parler de (to talk about)

Nous parlons du film. → Nous en parlons.

We're talking about the film. → We're talking about it.

se souvenir de (to remember)

Je me souviens de cette histoire. → Je m'en souviens.

I remember this story. → I remember it.

avoir peur de (to be afraid of)

Elle a peur des araignées. → Elle en a peur.

She's afraid of spiders. → She's afraid of them.

2 En Replaces Quantities

When replacing a quantity, keep the number or quantity word but replace the noun with en.

Question

Combien de livres as-tu ?

How many books do you have?

Answer with En

J'en ai trois.

I have three (of them).

Tu veux un café ? → Tu en veux un ?

Do you want a coffee? → Do you want one?

J'ai mangé deux pommes. → J'en ai mangé deux.

I ate two apples. → I ate two (of them).

Elle a beaucoup d'amis. → Elle en a beaucoup.

She has many friends. → She has many (of them).

Nous avons acheté un kilo de tomates. → Nous en avons acheté un kilo.

We bought a kilo of tomatoes. → We bought a kilo (of them).

3 En Replaces Partitive Articles (du, de la, des)

Use en to replace nouns preceded by partitive articles meaning "some" or "any".

Question

Tu veux du pain ?

Do you want (some) bread?

Answer with En

Oui, j'en veux.

Yes, I want some.

Il y a de la confiture ? → Il y en a ?

Is there (any) jam? → Is there any?

Je bois du café le matin. → J'en bois le matin.

I drink coffee in the morning. → I drink some in the morning.

Nous avons des questions. → Nous en avons.

We have (some) questions. → We have some.

Common Expressions with En

J'en veux !

I want some!

Je n'en peux plus !

I can't take it anymore!

Je m'en vais.

I'm leaving.

Je m'en fiche.

I don't care.

Il en va de même.

The same goes for...

S'en aller

To go away / leave

Word Order & Placement

Where do Y and En go?

Y and en are placed before the verb they relate to (or the auxiliary in compound tenses).

Tense Example with Y Example with En
Present J'y vais. J'en veux.
Passé Composé J'y suis allé(e). J'en ai mangé.
Near Future Je vais y aller. Je vais en acheter.
Negative Je n'y vais pas. Je n'en veux pas.
Imperative (affirm.) Vas-y ! Prends-en !

Important Note: People vs. Things

Y and en replace things, not people. For people, use stressed pronouns (moi, toi, lui, elle, etc.) after the preposition.

For things:

Je pense à mon travail. → J'y pense.

For people:

Je pense à Marie. → Je pense à elle.

Quick Reference

A side-by-side comparison to help you choose the right pronoun.

Use Y when...

  • ✓ Replacing a place (meaning "there")
  • ✓ Replacing à + thing
  • ✓ Verbs like: aller à, penser à, répondre à, participer à, s'intéresser à

Memory tip:

Y = à (both have the "ah" sound!)

Use En when...

  • ✓ Replacing de + noun
  • ✓ Replacing quantities (keep the number!)
  • ✓ Replacing du, de la, des + noun
  • ✓ Verbs like: parler de, avoir besoin de, avoir peur de, se souvenir de

Memory tip:

En = de (en replaces de!)
