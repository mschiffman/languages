Step zero, before anything else

Korean letters snap together into blocks. That's the whole trick.

Hangul is an alphabet, not thousands of characters. Consonants (blue) and vowels (red) stack into one square block per syllable. Learn about forty letters and you can read every word on this site — and every word on the exam.

What the exam actually is

The EPS-TOPIK is the Korean test run by Korea's Ministry of Employment and Labor for the Employment Permit System. Passing it is the first gate to an E-9 work visa in manufacturing, construction, agriculture, or fishing. It tests only two skills — reading and listening. There is no speaking or writing section, which is why this course leans so hard on audio.

  • 20 + 20reading questions + listening questions
  • 25 + 25 mintime for each section
  • 200 pointsmaximum score (100 per section)
  • Multiple choiceevery question, taken on a computer (UBT/CBT)
Where questions come from: the exam draws on an official question bank built around the Standard Textbook for EPS-TOPIK, with heavy emphasis on workplace situations, safety signs and warnings, and everyday life in Korea. That's why this course teaches safety vocabulary before restaurant vocabulary.

Your path through this course

  1. Learn Hangul (1–2 weeks)

    Letters first, sounds always. Play every audio clip and say it back out loud. Don't touch vocabulary until you can sound out a block without romanization.

  2. Drop the romanization

    Romanization is scaffolding, and it lies to you (Korean "eo" is not "ee-oh"). Once the letters feel familiar, cover the roman letters with your hand and read the Hangul alone.

  3. Build survival & workplace vocabulary

    Greetings, workplace words, safety commands, numbers. These are the highest-frequency items in the question bank. Listen, repeat, then read the Korean without audio.

  4. Move to real practice questions

    When you can read the phrase pages comfortably, start the official self-study materials and question bank on eps.hrdkorea.or.kr and past-paper listening sets. Come back here whenever a letter or number trips you up.

How to use the audio: every ▶ button plays a native-speed clip. Listen once with eyes on the Korean, once with eyes closed, then say it aloud before moving on. Three passes, ten seconds. Your ear is half your score on this exam.
